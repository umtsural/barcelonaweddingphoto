Before you talk to any fotógrafo, have these details ready:

You don’t need every detail finalized. But having a general sense of your day helps the fotógrafo give you accurate advice and precios.

Define Your Photography Priorities

Every pareja values different things. Before your consultation, ask yourself: Qué matters most to you? - [ ] Artistic, editorial retratos (magazine-quality pareja shots) - [ ] Documentary, candid moments (real emotions, unposed interactions) - [ ] Comprehensive coverage (every guest, every moment, nothing missed) - [ ] Specific locations (Park Güell, Gothic Quarter, Sitges, you have a vision) - [ ] Quick entrega (you want photos fast for social media or thank-you cards) - [ ] Album design (you want a physical heirloom, not just digital files) - [ ] Video/photo integration (you want seamless collaboration with your videographer) Rank your top 3 priorities. This helps the fotógrafo understand what to emphasize and whether they’re the right fit.

Review Their Portfolio (Critically)

Don’t just scroll Instagram highluzs. Look for: Full boda galerías: - Do they have galerías from bodas similar to yours? (Same espacio type, guest count, season) - Is the quality consistent from start to finish? (Getting ready through last dance) - Cómo do they handle different luzing conditions? (Bright sun, dim churches, flash recepción) Specific skills you need: - If you want Park Güell retratos, do they have examples? - If you’re having a church ceremonia, can they handle low luz? - If you want documental candids, do their galerías show real moments? Editing estilo: - Is the color palette warm, cool, moody, bright? - Do skin tones look natural? - Is the estilo consistent, or does it vary wildly? Red flag: A portfolio with only 20 to 30 “best of” images and no full galerías.

Anyone can get lucky with a few shots. Consistency is what matters.

Prepare Your Questions

A consultation is a two-way interview. You’re evaluating the fotógrafo, but they’re also evaluating whether you’re a good fit for their estilo and approach. Here are the preguntas that matter most:

“Can you show me a full galería from a boda similar to mine?”

This is the most important pregunta. A highluz reel doesn’t tell you anything about consistency. Ask to see a full galería, getting ready, ceremonia, retratos, recepción, from a boda with a similar espacio, size, or season. Qué to listen for: - Willingness to share full galerías (transparency) - Enthusiasm about finding a relevant example (preparedness) - Confidence in their consistency (professionalism)

“Cómo would you describe your fotografía estilo?”

Listen for specificity, not buzzwords. “I’m a storyteller” tells you nothing. “I’m 70% documental, 30% editorial, I capture real moments but guide retratos with gentle direction” tells you everything. Qué to listen for: - Clear articulation of their approach - Honesty about what they do well (and what they don’t) - Alignment with your priorities from Step 2

“Cómo many Barcelona bodas have you photographed?”

Local knowledge matters. A fotógrafo who knows Barcelona understands: - The best luz at Park Güell at 7 PM vs. 10 AM - Cómo to navigate Gothic Quarter crowds - Which espacios have fotografía restrictions - Cómo Barcelona’s late dinner culture affects timings Qué to listen for: - Specific espacio names and experiencias - Knowledge of Barcelona’s unique logistics - Comfort with the city’s rhythm and culture

“Walk me through how you work on a boda day.”

This reveals their process, personality, and professionalism. Do they: - Arrive early and scout? - Blend in or direct constantly? - Have a backup plan for equipment failure? - Coordinate with other vendors? Qué to listen for: - A clear, organized workflow - Adaptability and problem-solving - Respect for your experiencia (not just the photos)

“Cómo do you handle low luz or challenging conditions?”

Barcelona bodas often involve: - Dim church interiors - Evening recepcións with ambient luzing - Sunset retratos that transition to darkness - Sudden weather changes Qué to listen for: - Specific equipment (fast lenses, quality flash, backup cameras) - Techniques (bounce flash, high ISO with modern cameras, natural luz mastery) - Confidence, not hesitation

“Qué’s your approach to family formals?”

Family photos can be efficient and enjoyable, or they can be chaotic and stressful. Ask about: - Cómo they organize large groups - Cómo long they typically take - Whether they work from a shot list - Cómo they handle family dynamics Qué to listen for: - Efficiency (20 minutes or less for standard combinations) - Organization (pre-planned shot list) - People skills (calm under family pressure)

“Cómo many edited photos will we receive, and when?”

Be specific. “A few hundred” isn’t an answer. You want: - A range (e.g., “400 to 600 for 8 hours of coverage”) - A entrega timing (e.g., “4 to 6 weeks for full galería”) - Whether sneak peeks are included Qué to listen for: - Realistic timings (not “2 weeks” for 800 photos, that’s impossible with quality editing) - Transparency about the editing process - Clear communication about what’s included

“Qué’s included in your packages, and what costes extra?”

Hidden fees are the fastest way to sour a fotógrafo-client relationship. Ask about: - Hours of coverage - Second shooter - Engagement session - Travel fees (if your espacio is outside Barcelona) - Album design and printing - Raw files - Overtime rates Qué to listen for: - Clear, upfront precios - No vague “it depends” answers - A willingness to customize within reason

“Do you offer payment plans?”

Most professional fotógrafos require a retainer (typically 30 to 50%) to secure your date, with the balance due before the boda. Ask about: - Retainer percentage - Payment schedule - Cancellation and refund policy - Whether they accept credit cards or international transfers Qué to listen for: - Reasonable terms - Flexibility for international parejas - Clear contract language

“Qué happens if you get sick or can’t make it?”

This is non-negotiable. Every professional fotógrafo should have: - A backup fotógrafo network - Equipment redundancy - Insurance - A clear contingency plan Qué to listen for: - Specific names of backup fotógrafos - Equipment backup details - Insurance confirmation - Calm confidence (not nervous deflection)

“Cómo do you back up our photos?”

Your boda photos are irreplaceable. Ask about: - In-camera backup (dual memory cards) - Immediate backup after the boda (within 24 hours) - Cloud storage - Cómo long files are kept Qué to listen for: - Multiple backup systems - Fast turnaround on initial backup - Long-term file retention

“Cómo do you work with videographers?”

If you have a videographer (or plan to hire one), this matters. A fotógrafo who: - Communicates with the videographer beforehand - Coordinates angles and timing - Respects the videographer’s needs - Doesn’t compete for position Qué to listen for: - Positive, collaborative language - Experience working with videographers - Willingness to coordinate

“Can we give you a shot list or specific requests?”

A good fotógrafo welcomes your input while maintaining artistic freedom. Ask about: - Must-have family combinations - Special details or heirlooms - Surprise moments you’re planning - Cultural or religious requirements Qué to listen for: - Openness to your vision - Confidence in their ability to deliver - Balance between your requests and their expertise

“Qué do you love most about photographing bodas?”

This reveals their motivation. Are they in it for: - The art? - The connection with parejas? - The storytelling? - The business? Qué to listen for: - Genuine passion (not rehearsed sales pitch) - Alignment with your values - A reason that resonates with you

“Por qué should we choose you over another Barcelona fotógrafo?”

This is the closing pregunta. It forces them to articulate their unique value. Qué to listen for: - Specific skills, not generic claims - Honesty about their strengths and ideal clients - A reason that genuinely differentiates them

During the Consultation: Qué to Observe

Beyond the preguntas, pay attention to:

Their Communication Style

Do they listen more than they talk? Do they ask about your vision, or just sell their packages? Do they respond thoughtfully, or rush to the next topic?

Their Energy

Do they seem genuinely excited about your boda? Do they have ideas and suggestions? Do you feel comfortable with them?

Their Professionalism

Are they punctual (if in-person) or prepared (if virtual)? Do they have a contract ready to review? Do they follow up promptly after the consultation?

The Gut Check

After the consultation, ask yourself: - Did I feel heard? Or did they just pitch their packages? - Did I see myself in their work? Or did it feel generic? - Would I enjoy spending 8 to 12 hours with this person on my boda day? - Do I trust them to handle the unexpected?

Preguntas frecuentes
Cómo long should a boda fotografía consultation last? 30 to 45 minutes is ideal. Long enough to cover the essentials, short enough to respect everyone’s time. My consultations typically run 45 minutes because I want to really understand your vision.

Should both partners attend the consultation? Yes, if possible. Wedding fotografía is a joint decision, and both partners should feel comfortable with the fotógrafo. If one partner can’t make it, I recommend a follow-up call with the other partner.

Can we do the consultation virtually? Absolutely. Most of my consultations are video calls, especially for international parejas. I use Zoom, Google Meet, or QuésApp video, whatever works for you.

Qué if we’re not ready to book yet? No pressure. I encourage parejas to consult with multiple fotógrafos, compare estilos and personalities, and make a decision when they’re ready. I don’t use high-pressure tactics, ever.

Can we meet in person before booking? Of course. If you’re in Barcelona (or visiting), I’d love to meet for coffee or a drink. There’s no substitute for face-to-face connection.

Qué if we decide you’re not the right fit? That’s completely okay. Not every fotógrafo is right for every pareja. If I feel I’m not the best match for your vision, I’ll tell you honestly and recommend colleagues who might be a better fit.
