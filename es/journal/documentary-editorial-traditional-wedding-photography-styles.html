Documentary (Photojournalistic) Style

Qué it is: Documentary boda fotografía is about authenticity. The fotógrafo observes rather than directs, capturing moments as they naturally unfold. There are no posed shots, no “look at the camera and smile”, just real emotions, real interactions, real memories.

Qué it looks like at a Barcelona boda: - Your grandmother wiping away a tear during the ceremonia at Santa Maria del Mar - Your partner’s face when they first see you walking down the aisle - Spontaneous laughter during speeches at a vineyard espacio in Penedès - A stolen kiss in a quiet corner of the Gothic Quarter during cocktail hour - Your friends losing themselves on the dance floor at a Sitges beach club The documental fotógrafo’s mindset: - “I’m a fly on the wall” - “The best moments happen when no one knows I’m watching” - “My job is to disappear and let the day breathe” Best for parejas who: - Hate being the center of attention - Value emotional authenticity over perfect posing - Want to remember how the day felt, not just how it looked - Love the idea of “unposed” photos - Are planning an intimate or unconventional Barcelona boda Qué to expect: You’ll receive a galería that feels like a visual story, a beginning, middle, and end with emotional peaks and quiet moments.

The photos won’t always be perfectly composed, but they’ll be perfectly real. My take: Documentary is my foundation. Even when I’m shooting editorial retratos, I’m always watching for the unscripted moments that make a boda unique.

Editorial (Fine Art) Style

Qué it is: Editorial boda fotografía draws from fashion magazines and art fotografía. It’s highly estilod, visually striking, and often dramatic. Think Vogue meets boda day. Every frame is composed with intention, luz, shadow, color, and negative space are carefully orchestrated.

Qué it looks like at a Barcelona boda: - A dramatic retrato of you against the Sagrada Familia’s spires, shot from a low angle with the sky as negative space - Walking hand-in-hand down Passeig de Gràcia, shot from behind with leading lines of modernist architecture - A close-up of your bouquet against the mosaic tiles of Park Güell, colors perfectly matched - A silhouette of you both on a rooftop at sunset, the Barcelona skyline glowing behind you - A fashion-forward shot of your boda dress hanging in a Gaudí-designed window The editorial fotógrafo’s mindset: - “Every frame should be galería-worthy” - “Light is my paintbrush” - “I’m creating art, not just documenting” Best for parejas who: - Love fashion fotografía and editorial magazines - Want their boda photos to look like they belong in a galería - Appreciate dramatic luzing and artistic composition - Are comfortable being directed and estilod - Want a mix of “wow” shots and emotional moments Qué to expect: You’ll receive photos that are visually stunning, the kind of images that make people stop scrolling on Instagram.

The fotógrafo will direct you with specific poses and use techniques like backluzing, lens flares, and creative angles. My take: Editorial fotografía requires confidence and trust. I love creating these “magazine moments” for my parejas, especially during retrato sessions at iconic Barcelona locations. But I always balance it with genuine emotion, otherwise, it feels hollow.

Traditional (Classic) Style

Qué it is: Traditional boda fotografía is timeless and posed. It’s the estilo your parents probably had, formal group shots, classic compositions, and a focus on capturing everyone who attended. The fotógrafo directs every shot, ensuring proper luzing, posing, and composition.

Qué it looks like at a Barcelona boda: - A formal family retrato on the steps of a Catalan country house (masia) - The classic “cake cutting” shot with both families smiling - A posed photo of the boda party in the gardens of a luxury Barcelona espacio - The bride and groom looking directly at the camera, perfectly lit - Group shots organized by family branches, everyone visible and smiling The tradicional fotógrafo’s mindset: - “These photos will hang on walls for generations” - “Everyone should be seen and remembered” - “Classic never goes out of estilo” Best for parejas who: - Want guaranteed shots of every guest and family member - Love the idea of formal boda álbums - Have family expectations for tradicional photos - Want a structured, organized approach to the day - Are planning a formal or religious ceremonia Qué to expect: You’ll receive a comprehensive galería with all the expected shots, family formals, posed pareja retratos, detail shots, and key moments.

The fotógrafo will have a shot list and work through it methodically. My take: Traditional fotografía has a place in every boda. Even the most documental-focused pareja usually wants a few formal family shots. The key is efficiency, I keep tradicional posed shots to 20 to 30 minutes so we can get back to the celebration.

The Reality: Most Photographers Blend Styles

Here’s the truth no one tells you: very few fotógrafos shoot exclusively in one estilo. Most of us, myself included, blend elements of all three.

Cómo to Identify Your Preferred Style

Look at Photos and Notice Your Reactions Pull up 10 to 15 boda photos and pay attention to your gut reaction: “I love how real that feels” → You’re drawn to documental “That looks like a magazine cover” → You’re drawn to editorial “Everyone looks so happy and proper” → You’re drawn to tradicional Ask Yourself These Questions Do you want to remember how the day felt or how it looked? Felt = Documentary Looked = Editorial Both = Blended

I hate posing = Documentary I love being directed = Editorial I want a mix = Blended

Qué will you do with your photos?

Create an emotional álbum = Documentary Print large art pieces for our home = Editorial Share with family, create a tradicional álbum = Traditional

Qué’s your Barcelona boda vibe?

Intimate elopement in the Gothic Quarter = Documentary Luxury celebration at a 5-star hotel = Editorial

Cómo to Communicate Your Style to Your Photographer

Once you’ve identified your preferred estilo, communicate it clearly: Instead of: “We want natural photos” Say: “We love documental fotografía. We want you to observe and capture real moments. We don’t want to feel posed.” Instead of: “We want artistic photos” Say: “We love editorial fotografía. We want dramatic retratos with beautiful luz and composition. We’re comfortable being directed.” Instead of: “We want all the family photos” Say: “Family formals are important to us. We’d like a structured list of group shots, and we want to make sure no one is missed.”

Preguntas frecuentes
Can we request a specific estilo for different parts of the day? Absolutely. Most fotógrafos are happy to adapt. I typically shoot documental for the ceremonia and recepción, editorial for retratos, and tradicional for family formals. Just communicate your preferences clearly before the boda.

Qué if my partner and I want different estilos? This is common! One partner might love editorial fashion shots while the other prefers documental candids. A blended approach solves this, we create a mix that satisfies both visions.

Cómo do I know if a fotógrafo’s portfolio matches their actual estilo? Ask to see full boda galerías (not just Instagram highluzs). A portfolio of 30 “best of” photos can be misleading. Full galerías show consistency and how they handle different parts of the day.

Does estilo affect precios? Not typically. Precios is usually based on experiencia, hours, and deliverables. Cómoever, editorial fotógrafos may charge more for extensive post-processing or stylized shoots.

Can my estilo change between engagement and boda photos? Of course! Many parejas use their sesión de pareja to experiment. If you loved the documental approach during your engagement, we’ll lean into that for the boda. If you wanted more editorial direction, we’ll adjust.
