Before the Wedding: The Foundation of Trust

6 to 12 Months Before: Planificación & Vision Long before your boda day, we’re building a relationship. This phase includes: Initial consultation: A video call where we discuss your vision, priorities, and concerns. I show you full galerías (not just highluzs) so you understand my complete approach. Venue walkthrough: If possible, I visit your Barcelona espacio beforehand to scout luz, angles, and backup locations. For destination parejas, I do this virtually or upon arrival. Timeline collaboration: I work with your planner (or directly with you) to build a fotografía-friendly timing. This isn’t about me, it’s about ensuring you have time for the moments that matter without feeling rushed. Engagement session: Most of my packages include this.

It’s a chance for us to work together before the big day, for you to get comfortable in front of my camera, and for me to learn your best angles. Qué parejas say: “The sesión de pareja made us so much more relaxed on the boda day. We already knew how you worked, and it felt like having a friend there.” 2 to 4 Weeks Before: Final Details As the date approaches, we finalize: Shot list: Not a rigid checklist, but a conversation about must-have shots. Family formals, special heirlooms, surprise moments you’re planning. Vendor coordination: I connect with your planner, videographer, and other vendors to ensure seamless collaboration. Weather contingency: Barcelona is reliably sunny, but we plan indoor backup locations just in case.

Emergency contact: You get my personal QuésApp for any last-minute preguntas or changes.

Getting Ready (2 to 3 Hours Before Ceremony)

Qué I’m doing: - Arrive 15 minutes early to assess the space, set up backup equipment, and say hello - Photograph details: dress, shoes, rings, invitation suite, perfume, jewelry, handwritten vows - Capture candid moments: champagne with bridesmaids, nervous laughter, the quiet moment before the dress goes on - Document the “first look” with parents or bridesmaids if planned Qué you’re experiencing: - Minimal direction from me. I’m an observer, not a director - Occasional gentle prompts: “Can you hold the dress while your mother buttons it?” or “Look at each other and laugh, yes, exactly like that” - The realization that I’m not intrusive. I’m just… there My approach: The getting-ready phase sets the tone for the entire day. I want you to feel relaxed, not performative.

If you’re stressed, I give you space. If you’re joyful, I lean in.

First Look (If Applicable)

Qué I’m doing: - Scout the location 30 minutes beforehand for the best luz and privacy - Position myself discreetly, often with a long lens so I’m not in your space - Capture the raw emotion: the breath before, the turn, the reaction, the embrace - Give you 2 to 3 minutes of private time before stepping back in Qué you’re experiencing: - A private moment that feels like it’s just you two, even though I’m there - No awkward posing. Just real emotion - The relief of seeing each other before the ceremonia (if you chose a first look) Note: Only 40% of my Barcelona parejas do a first look. The other 60% prefer the tradicional aisle moment. Both are beautiful, I support whatever feels right for you.

Pre-Ceremony Portraits Qué I’m doing: - If you did a first look, we use this time for pareja retratos at 1 to 2 Barcelona locations - If not, I photograph the espacio details, guests arriving, and the anticipation building - Coordinate with the videographer so we’re never in each other’s shots Timing: - 45 to 60 minutes for pareja retratos (if first look) - 20 to 30 minutes for boda party photos - 15 to 20 minutes for family formals (if done pre-ceremonia)

The Ceremony

Qué I’m doing: - Arrive 30 minutes early to set up, test luzing, and find the best angles - During the ceremonia, I move quietly and strategically: - Wide shots: From the back, capturing the full scene - Close-ups: From the sides, capturing expressions without blocking guests - Detail shots: Rings, hands, programs, flowers - Guest reactions: Parents, siblings, friends wiping tears - Use silent shutter mode during vows and readings - Never use flash during the ceremonia unless the espacio is extremely dark (and even then, I bounce it discreetly) Qué you’re experiencing: - You won’t notice me. That’s the goal - If you do see me, it’s a brief, reassuring smile, then I disappear again - The ceremonia feels uninterrupted and sacred My ceremonia philosophy: This is your moment, not my photoshoot.

I’m there to witness and preserve, not to perform.

Post-Ceremony: The Golden Hour Qué I’m doing: - Immediately after the ceremonia, we do family formals (if not done pre-ceremonia) - Then: pareja retratos during golden hour, the most beautiful luz of the day - I guide you to the best Barcelona locations based on your espacio and the luz - I give gentle direction: “Walk toward me and look at each other,” “Whisper something that makes her laugh,” “Hold hands and look at the view” Qué you’re experiencing: - 30 to 45 minutes that feels like a mini-date in the middle of your boda - You’re not “posing”, you’re being yourselves in a beautiful place - I handle the logistics (crowds, timing, luz) so you can just be present Golden hour in Barcelona: Depending on the season, this is 6:00 to 8:00 PM (spring/summer) or 4:00 to 6:00 PM (autumn/winter).

I build your timing around this window. Cocktail Hour & Reception Qué I’m doing: - During cocktail hour: candid guest interactions, detail shots of food and drinks, espacio ambiance - During dinner: table settings, speeches, reactions, the “in-between” moments - During dancing: I’m on the dance floor, capturing energy, movement, joy - Late night: the quiet moments, a pareja stepping outside for air, the last dance, the goodbye hugs Qué you’re experiencing: - I’m everywhere and nowhere. One moment I’m photographing your grandmother’s laugh, the next I’m invisible - I don’t interrupt conversations or pull you away from guests - If something special happens (a surprise toast, an impromptu dance), I’m already there

Qué Makes Working with Me Different

I’ve photographed bodas in Barcelona for over a decade. Here’s what parejas consistently tell me sets me apart:

I’m Calm Under Pressure

Weddings rarely go exactly to plan. The schedule runs late, it rains, a vendor cancels. Cuándo chaos happens, I become the calmest person in the room. I’ve seen it all, and I know how to adapt without panicking. Real example: A pareja’s outdoor ceremonia in Sitges was hit by a sudden thunderstorm. Within 10 minutes, I’d coordinated with the espacio to move everything indoors, adjusted my luzing setup, and captured photos that looked intentional, not rushed. The pareja said it was their favorite part of the day.

I Know Barcelona Intimately

I don’t just know the famous spots. I know: - Which streets in the Gothic Quarter have the best luz at 6 PM - Dónde to park near Park Güell so we don’t waste time - The quiet café where you can take a breather between locations - The exact moment when the Sagrada Familia facade glows at sunset This local knowledge saves you time, reduces stress, and creates better photos.

I Care About Your Experience, Not Just the Photos

Yes, I want to create beautiful images. But more than that, I want you to enjoy your boda day. If that means putting down my camera to help fix your veil, or giving you a moment of privacy when emotions run high, I do it. The best photos come from genuine joy, not forced poses. My job is to create the conditions for that joy.

Common Concerns Addressed

“Will you be in our guests’ way?” No. I position myself strategically, often at the sides or back of the ceremonia, and use long lenses to capture close-ups without being close. During the recepción, I blend in like a guest with a camera. “Qué if we’re awkward in front of the camera?” Most people are. That’s why the sesión de pareja is so valuable, it builds comfort. On the boda day, I use gentle direction and prompts that feel natural, not posed. By the time we get to retratos, you’ll forget the camera is there. “Cómo do you handle family drama?” With discretion and professionalism. If tensions arise, I focus on the positive moments and give space when needed. I’ve mediated family photo logistics countless times, I know how to get everyone organized without adding stress.

“Qué if we don’t like being photographed during dinner?” I respect your boundaries completely. If you prefer I don’t photograph during dinner, I’ll focus on espacio details, speeches, and candid guest moments. We discuss this beforehand so there are no surprises.

The Bottom Line

Working with a professional fotógrafo de bodas en Barcelona isn’t just about hiring someone with a good camera. It’s about trusting someone to: See your day with artistic vision Handle the logistics so you don’t have to worry Preserve your memories with technical excellence Respect your experiencia above all else If you’re planning a Barcelona boda and want to know more about how I work, let’s schedule a call. I’ll walk you through a full boda galería, answer every pregunta, and help you feel confident in your choice. Your boda day deserves a fotógrafo who feels like a trusted friend, not a vendor with a checklist.

Preguntas frecuentes
Cómo early do you arrive on the boda day? I arrive 15 to 30 minutes before my contracted start time to assess the space, set up equipment, and say hello without cutting into your coverage.

Do you eat during the boda? Yes, and I appreciate it when parejas include me in vendor meals. A fed fotógrafo is a happy fotógrafo. I eat quickly and discreetly during dinner service so I’m ready for speeches and dancing.

Do you work with videographers? Absolutely. I collaborate seamlessly with videographers, coordinating angles and timing so we complement each other rather than compete. I have preferred videographers I can recommend if you haven’t booked one yet.

Qué happens if you get sick? I have a trusted network of backup fotógrafos in Barcelona. In 10+ years, I’ve never missed a boda, but I have a detailed contingency plan in place.

Can we request specific shots? Of course. I encourage parejas to share their vision, must-have family combinations, and special details. I balance your requests with my artistic judgment to create a galería that feels both personal and professional.
